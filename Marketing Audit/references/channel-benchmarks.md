# Channel Benchmarks & Scoring Rubric

Use these tables to score each of the 12 audit categories as **Strong**, **Moderate**, **Weak**, or **Critical**.
A category is **Critical** when data is missing entirely or when 3+ metrics fall in the Weak column.

---

## 1. SEO & AI Discoverability

| Metric                        | Strong                        | Moderate                    | Weak / Critical                |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Domain authority (Moz/Ahrefs) | > 40                          | 20–40                       | < 20                           |
| Ranking keywords (top 100)    | > 500                         | 100–500                     | < 100                          |
| Meta titles & descriptions    | Unique per page, keyword-rich | Some pages missing           | Generic or auto-generated      |
| XML sitemap & robots.txt      | Present, error-free           | Present with issues          | Missing                        |
| Structured data / schema      | Implemented on key pages      | Partial                      | None                           |
| Google Business Profile       | Claimed, optimized, 20+ reviews | Claimed, partial info      | Not claimed or absent          |

**Best practices:**
- Every page has a unique meta title (50–60 chars) and description (140–160 chars)
- Structured data markup on retreat listing pages, reviews, and FAQs
- Google Business Profile fully completed with photos, hours, services
- Technical SEO fundamentals: canonical tags, hreflang (if multilingual), clean URL structure

## 2. Homepage Messaging

| Metric                           | Strong                          | Moderate                      | Weak / Critical                 |
|----------------------------------|---------------------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Headline clarity                 | Instantly conveys what they do  | Somewhat clear, jargon-light  | Vague, generic, or missing      |
| Value proposition                | Unique, specific, benefit-led   | Present but generic           | Missing or buried               |
| Target audience clarity          | Speaks directly to ideal guest  | Broad but relevant            | No clear audience addressed     |
| Emotional hook                   | Creates desire, paints outcome  | Informational but flat        | No emotional connection         |
| Above-fold completeness          | Headline + CTA + visual in view | Missing one element           | Missing two or more             |

**Best practices:**
- Headline answers "What is this, who is it for, and why should I care?" within 5 seconds
- Sub-headline reinforces with specific benefit or transformation promise
- Hero image/video shows the retreat experience, not stock photography
- At least one primary CTA visible above the fold

## 3. Retreat Positioning

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Unique differentiator           | Clear niche or specialty stated   | Some differentiation implied   | Looks like every other retreat   |
| Retreat type/style clarity      | Specific: yoga, silent, wellness  | Mentioned but not emphasized   | Unclear what kind of retreat     |
| Ideal guest avatar              | Explicitly describes who it's for | Vaguely implied                | No targeting evident             |
| Transformation promise          | States what guests leave with     | Hints at outcomes              | Only describes logistics         |
| Pricing transparency            | Visible, contextualized           | Available on request only      | No pricing information           |

**Best practices:**
- Clearly states the type of retreat and who it's designed for
- Articulates the transformation or outcome (not just activities and amenities)
- Addresses common objections (safety, solo travel, experience level)
- Differentiates from competitors with a unique angle or philosophy

## 4. Trust & Social Proof

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Testimonials / reviews          | 10+ with photos/names/detail     | 3–9 testimonials present       | 0–2 or only anonymous            |
| External review presence        | Google, TripAdvisor, Trustpilot   | Present on 1 platform          | No external reviews              |
| Average rating                  | 4.5+ stars                        | 4.0–4.4 stars                  | Below 4.0 or no ratings          |
| Media mentions / press          | Featured in recognized outlets    | Some mentions or blog features | None found                       |
| Certifications / affiliations   | Industry certs, yoga alliance, etc | Some credentials shown        | No trust badges                  |
| Case studies / outcomes         | Before/after or detailed stories  | Brief success mentions         | None                             |

**Best practices:**
- Testimonials include guest name, photo, retreat attended, and specific outcome
- Reviews displayed prominently on homepage, not buried in a sub-page
- Responds to all Google/TripAdvisor reviews (positive and negative)
- Shows real guest photos and videos, not stock images

## 5. Booking Funnel Experience

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| CTA visibility                  | Primary CTA on every key page    | CTA on homepage only           | No clear booking CTA             |
| Booking steps                   | 3 steps or fewer to complete     | 4–5 steps                     | 6+ steps or confusing flow       |
| Payment options                 | Multiple (card, PayPal, deposit) | Card only                      | Inquiry-only, no online booking  |
| Date/availability display       | Calendar with live availability  | Dates listed, no live status   | No dates or "contact us"         |
| Abandonment recovery            | Exit-intent popup or email follow-up | One recovery mechanism      | None                             |
| Post-booking confirmation       | Instant email + prep guide       | Confirmation email only        | No automated confirmation        |

**Best practices:**
- "Book Now" or "Reserve Your Spot" CTA visible on every retreat page
- Booking flow requires 3 clicks or fewer from landing page to payment
- Offers deposit option (not just full payment) to lower commitment barrier
- Shows scarcity signals: remaining spots, early-bird deadlines

## 6. Mobile Optimization

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Mobile-responsive layout        | Fully responsive, no horizontal scroll | Minor layout issues       | Not responsive or broken         |
| Touch target size               | All buttons/links > 44px         | Most adequate                  | Small, hard-to-tap elements      |
| Mobile page speed (LCP)         | < 2.5s                           | 2.5–4.0s                      | > 4.0s                           |
| Mobile navigation               | Hamburger menu, clear hierarchy  | Functional but cluttered       | Broken or confusing              |
| Mobile booking flow             | Fully functional on mobile       | Works but awkward              | Breaks or requires desktop       |
| Google Mobile-Friendly Test     | Pass, no errors                  | Pass with warnings             | Fail                             |

**Best practices:**
- All content readable without pinching/zooming
- Forms use appropriate mobile input types (tel, email, date pickers)
- Images lazy-loaded and properly sized for mobile viewports
- Sticky CTA button on mobile retreat pages

## 7. Speed & Performance

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)  | < 2.5s                           | 2.5–4.0s                      | > 4.0s                           |
| First Input Delay (FID)         | < 100ms                          | 100–300ms                     | > 300ms                          |
| Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)   | < 0.1                            | 0.1–0.25                      | > 0.25                           |
| Image optimization              | WebP/AVIF, lazy-loaded, sized    | Some optimization              | Uncompressed, oversized images   |
| Server response (TTFB)          | < 200ms                          | 200–600ms                     | > 600ms                          |
| Core Web Vitals (overall)       | All 3 "Good"                     | 1–2 "Good"                    | 0 "Good"                         |

**Best practices:**
- All images served in modern formats (WebP/AVIF) with appropriate dimensions
- CSS and JS minified, with critical CSS inlined
- CDN configured for static assets
- Browser caching headers set for at least 1 year on static files

## 8. Branding Consistency

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Visual identity across channels | Same logo, colors, fonts everywhere | Minor inconsistencies       | Different branding per platform  |
| Tone of voice                   | Consistent personality throughout | Mostly consistent              | Wildly different tone per channel|
| Profile completeness            | Bio, link, logo, cover on all    | Partial on 1–2 platforms       | Missing on most platforms        |
| Photography style               | Consistent, professional, on-brand | Mixed quality                 | Stock photos or inconsistent     |
| Brand story presence            | About page + woven into content  | About page only                | No brand story found             |

**Best practices:**
- Logo, color palette, and typography consistent across website, social, and email
- Brand voice guidelines evident: same tone on Instagram captions, website copy, and emails
- Professional photography that reflects the actual retreat experience
- Bio/description consistent across all social profiles with matching links

## 9. Conversion Opportunities

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| CTAs per page                   | 2–3 relevant CTAs per key page   | 1 CTA per page                 | Pages with no CTA                |
| Lead magnet presence            | Guide, quiz, or free resource    | Newsletter signup only         | No lead capture mechanism        |
| Urgency/scarcity triggers       | Limited spots, countdown, early-bird | One trigger present          | No urgency elements              |
| Social proof near CTAs          | Testimonial or review near CTA   | Somewhere on same page         | No social proof near decisions   |
| Exit-intent strategy            | Popup with relevant offer        | Generic popup                  | No exit-intent capture           |
| Retargeting pixel installed     | Facebook + Google pixels active  | One pixel installed            | No retargeting setup             |

**Best practices:**
- Every retreat page has a primary CTA ("Book Now") and secondary CTA ("Ask a Question")
- Lead magnet offered (retreat packing guide, meditation guide, etc.) for email capture
- Scarcity signals: "Only 4 spots left" or "Early-bird ends [date]"
- Retargeting campaigns follow up with site visitors within 7 days

## 10. Content & Storytelling

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Blog / resource presence        | Active blog, 4+ posts/month     | 1–3 posts/month               | No blog or < 1 post/month       |
| Content depth                   | 1,200+ words, well-structured   | 600–1,200 words               | < 600 words, thin content        |
| Visual storytelling             | Photos, video, guest stories     | Some visual content            | Text-only or stock imagery       |
| Social media content            | 4–7 posts/week, mixed formats   | 2–3 posts/week                | < 2 posts/week                   |
| Video presence                  | Regular retreat videos, reels    | Occasional video               | No video content                 |
| Story-driven messaging          | Guest transformation narratives  | Activity descriptions only     | Pure logistics, no storytelling  |

**Best practices:**
- Blog content targets retreat-related search intent (e.g., "how to choose a yoga retreat")
- Guest stories and transformation journeys shared as blog posts and social content
- Video content showcases the retreat environment, activities, and testimonials
- Content repurposed across blog, social, email, and YouTube

## 11. Email Capture Strategy

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Signup form visibility          | Homepage, blog, popup, footer    | Footer only                    | No signup form found             |
| Lead magnet / incentive         | Valuable freebie (guide, discount)| "Subscribe for updates" only  | No incentive offered             |
| Welcome sequence                | 3–5 email automated series       | Single welcome email           | No automation                    |
| Signup form design              | Branded, clear value proposition | Basic but functional           | Generic or broken                |
| Segmentation signals            | Collects interest/retreat type   | Name + email only              | Email only                       |
| List growth strategy            | Multiple capture points + content| One capture method             | No active list building          |

**Best practices:**
- Lead magnet aligns with retreat audience: packing guide, meditation starter kit, retreat quiz
- Welcome sequence introduces the brand, shares guest stories, and offers early-bird pricing
- Signup forms appear on homepage, each blog post, and as timed or exit-intent popup
- Collects at least one preference data point (retreat type, travel dates, experience level)

## 12. Google & AI Search Readiness

| Metric                          | Strong                            | Moderate                       | Weak / Critical                  |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Schema markup                   | FAQ, Review, Event, Product      | Basic schema only              | No structured data               |
| Content for AI snippets         | FAQ sections, Q&A, how-to guides | Some structured content        | No snippet-ready content         |
| Conversational content          | Answers natural-language queries | Some Q&A content               | Only transactional copy          |
| Featured snippet eligibility    | Appears in or formatted for snippets | Partially eligible          | No snippet-eligible content      |
| Voice search readiness          | Content matches spoken queries   | Some alignment                 | Not optimized for voice          |
| AI overview / SGE presence      | Content cited in AI responses    | Some relevance                 | Not referenced                   |

**Best practices:**
- FAQ schema on retreat pages addressing common questions (What to bring? Is it beginner-friendly?)
- Content structured with clear headings, concise answers, and numbered lists
- Targets conversational long-tail keywords ("best yoga retreat for beginners in Bali")
- Review and Event schema on retreat listing pages for rich search results
- Content designed to be cited by AI overviews (authoritative, well-structured, factual)

---

## Overall Health Scoring

Derive an **Overall Marketing Health** label from the 12 category scores:

- **Strong**: 8+ categories Strong, no Critical
- **Moderate**: 5–7 categories Strong, at most 1 Critical
- **Weak**: Fewer than 5 Strong, or 3+ Weak/Critical
- **Critical**: 6+ categories Weak or Critical
