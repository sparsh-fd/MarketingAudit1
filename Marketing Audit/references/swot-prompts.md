# Competitor SWOT Research Prompts

Use this file during Step 5 (Competitor Research) and Step 7.5 (Competitor SWOT).
Replace `[Competitor]` with the competitor name and `[Business]` with the audited business name.

---

## 1. Web Searches to Run Per Competitor

Run these searches for each competitor. Focus on retreat and wellness industry signals.

1. `"[Competitor]" reviews customers retreat` — surface guest sentiment, complaints, praise
2. `"[Competitor]" vs "[Business]"` — find direct comparison content, forums, Reddit threads
3. `site:[competitor-domain] blog OR resources OR guides` — assess content marketing depth
4. `"[Competitor]" pricing retreat packages` — understand pricing model and positioning
5. `"[Competitor]" instagram OR linkedin OR facebook followers` — find social presence data
6. `"[Competitor]" partnership OR certification OR yoga alliance` — identify strategic advantages
7. `"[Competitor]" launched OR announces OR new retreat [current year]` — recent momentum
8. `"[Competitor]" booking process OR reserve spot` — evaluate their booking funnel

---

## 2. SWOT Mapping Guide

Answer these questions using the research gathered above. Every answer must cite
specific evidence — never write a SWOT quadrant from assumption alone.

### Strengths (competitor advantages over [Business])

1. What does the competitor rank higher for in search results, and for which retreat-related keywords?
2. Which social platforms does the competitor dominate where [Business] is absent or weaker?
3. Does the competitor have stronger trust signals — more reviews, higher ratings, notable press, certifications?
4. Is their content strategy more developed — more blog posts, guest stories, retreat videos?
5. Do they have a clearer retreat positioning or unique niche that [Business] lacks?
6. Is their booking funnel smoother — fewer steps, deposit options, live availability calendar?
7. Do they have stronger email capture — lead magnets, welcome sequences, popup opt-ins?

### Weaknesses (where the competitor falls short)

1. What are the most common guest complaints in reviews (Google, TripAdvisor, Trustpilot)?
2. Which marketing channels are they neglecting — inactive social accounts, no blog, no email capture?
3. Is their website slow, poorly designed, or missing mobile optimization?
4. Is their homepage messaging vague — unclear who the retreat is for or what transformation it offers?
5. Is their pricing opaque, uncompetitive, or poorly communicated?
6. Do they have branding inconsistencies across channels (different logos, bios, photography style)?
7. Is their booking process broken, confusing, or inquiry-only with no online booking?

### Opportunities (gaps [Business] can exploit)

1. Which retreat niches or audience segments does the competitor ignore that [Business] could own?
2. Are there content topics the competitor hasn't covered that have search demand?
3. Which platforms does the competitor neglect where the target audience is active?
4. Are guests publicly complaining about competitor shortcomings that [Business] already solves?
5. Is there a pricing tier or booking model the competitor doesn't offer (deposit, payment plans)?
6. Are there geographic markets or retreat types the competitor hasn't addressed?
7. Is the competitor weak on AI/voice search readiness, creating an opportunity for [Business]?

### Threats (risks [Business] should monitor)

1. Is the competitor growing rapidly — new locations, expanding retreat calendar, hiring?
2. Are they investing heavily in content or paid ads that could crowd out [Business] in search?
3. Do they have a significantly larger social following or community that creates network effects?
4. Are they forming partnerships with wellness platforms, travel agencies, or influencers?
5. Is their brand perception shifting positively based on recent reviews or press coverage?
6. Could their pricing changes or new retreat offerings directly undercut [Business]'s positioning?

## 4. Fallback: Finding Proxy Competitors

When direct competitors cannot be identified from the website or search results,
use these approaches to find proxy competitors:

1. **Retreat directory search**: Search `top [retreat type] retreats [year]` or `best [wellness/yoga/meditation] retreats`
2. **Alternative-to search**: Search `[Business] alternatives` or `retreats like [Business]`
3. **SERP competitors**: Take the top 3 non-directory results ranking for [Business]'s primary keyword
4. **Review platform neighbors**: Check who appears alongside [Business] on TripAdvisor, BookRetreats, or retreat directory category pages
5. **Social adjacency**: Search the same hashtags [Business] uses and find accounts with larger followings in the retreat niche

When using proxy competitors, note clearly in the report:
> "Direct competitors could not be confirmed. The following retreat providers are used as
> benchmarking proxies and may not represent direct competitive threats."

Select proxies that match [Business] on at least 2 of these dimensions:
- Same retreat type or wellness focus
- Similar target audience (demographic, experience level, geography)
- Comparable company size or retreat capacity
- Similar price range
